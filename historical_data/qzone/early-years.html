Early Years

400 posts on Qzone, Apr 2007 – Aug 2016

I. The Builder Apr–Dec 2007 · 129 posts

The first post lands on April 19, 2007: 谁更能控制自己，谁就更能控制命运 — whoever controls themselves better controls their destiny. It reads like a self-pep-talk from a young man about to leap. That same day, three more posts: one comparing Eastern and Western systems of governance, one on evaluating business projects, one on Ming dynasty trade policy. All filed under "学习笔记" (study notes). He is 20-something, freshly out of Zhejiang University, and his mind is a firehose.

The Web Studio

By summer 2007, "刘氏兄弟" (Liu Brothers) is born — a two-man web design shop with his friend/colleague 小刘 (Little Liu). The posts become a live dev blog: building a PHP CMS from scratch, battling IE bugs, discovering AJAX's limitations for file upload, debating Flash vs. DHTML, building an online Photoshop tool in JavaScript. He writes about MVC architecture, Unicode encoding, JavaScript debugging in VS.NET, and CSS frameworks. The technical ambition is remarkable for a small studio.

做网站 2000元！各位多多捧场哦！ — Websites for ¥2000! Come support us!

The Hustler-Philosopher

Between coding sprints, he reads voraciously: 货币战争 (Currency Wars). He writes about credit money, game theory (the pirate gold problem), and why 科学技术是第一生产力 (science and technology are the primary productive force). He's not just building websites; he's building a worldview.

The Young Man

Woven through the tech and business posts is a full life: getting drunk with friends, photographing Hangzhou streets with a film camera, watching Rome over two afternoons, writing about love and loneliness. He quotes Zhuangzi: "相濡以沫，不如相忘于江湖" — better to forget each other in the rivers and lakes than to sustain each other with spit in a dried-up pond. He throws his first Home Party. He makes his cat jealous of his coding.

Key posts

  • 网站前台的超简单模板写法 (Jul 23) — The PHP template trick he later quotes in "Thank you, GitHub and Jekyll" as "my naive thoughts from 2007." This is the original source.
  • 你凭什么创业？？ (Jul 19) — "What gives you the right to start a business??" — a moment of self-doubt after a friend's harsh feedback.
  • LiuBros Rule No.1：客户精力不投入的网站不做 (Nov 2) — If the client won't invest their own effort, don't take the project.
  • 2008年，做好潜力股 (Dec 29) — Someone once called him a "potential stock." He vows to prove them right.

II. The E-Commerce Wave 2008–2009 · 142 posts

The studio evolves. In late 2008, the 淘宝宅男日记 (Taobao Introvert Diary) series begins — and never stops. What starts as an experiment selling Seven Wolves (七匹狼) leather goods on Taobao becomes a real business.

The Taobao Diaries

These are extraordinary business case studies in real time:

  • Diary 1 (Nov 2008): Studying Taobao's ranking algorithm, discovering "橱窗推荐" (showcase recommendations).
  • Diary 4 (Dec 2008): Visiting suppliers for the first time, moved by their work ethic. "看到他们一直忙到我们离开，心中振奋。"
  • Diary 5 (Jan 2009): Hitting diamond reputation. Noticing that higher reputation = less haggling from buyers.
  • Diary 11 (Apr 2009): Adding Cartelo (卡帝乐鳄鱼) brand, sales jump immediately.
  • Diary 14 (Apr 2009): Teaching himself Taobao direct marketing (直通车), writing tutorials.
  • Diary 21 (Nov 2009): Supplier agrees to exclusive distribution. "我们上道了" — we've made it to the track.
  • Diary 24 (Nov 2009): "放血疗法" — purging low-quality distributors like bloodletting.

The Growth

The studio is still running, but Taobao is taking over. He teaches himself product photography with a borrowed Nikon D60. He builds a distributor network, negotiates with brands, and starts thinking like a merchant instead of a developer. The ¥2000 website builder is becoming a real e-commerce operator.

Meanwhile, Life

His grandfather passes away (Mar 2008): "爷爷最后一次神志清醒的跟我促膝谈话的内容是劝我赶紧成家" — grandfather's last lucid conversation was urging him to start a family. He loses his phone and briefly considers never using one again. He starts an aquarium. He discovers 科幻大师 (Masters of Science Fiction) — "anything that gets Hawking to narrate must be worth watching."

The Thinker Doesn't Stop

Even while running the business, he reads quantum physics and concludes: 物质就是振动？ — matter is vibration? He'll carry this thread for years.

Key posts

  • 互联网上的消费品市场观察 (Sep 2008) — A remarkably mature analysis of China's internet consumer market, comparing it to a city of 250 million.
  • 一个好将领要学会如何应付败仗 (Oct 2008) — A good general must learn to handle defeat. More revealing than any victory post.
  • 这一年要结束了，新的一年开始了 (Jan 2009) — The studio brothers have all left for New Year. He eats instant noodles alone, counts the last few bills, and feels himself growing up in an elevator.

III. The Operator 2010–2011 · 49 posts

Writing slows sharply. The business demands full attention. The operation scales from a 32-sqm apartment to a 400-sqm warehouse. He moves from selling other brands' products to considering creating his own brand.

十年 (Jan 1, 2010) — "Ten Years." A reflective New Year's post. Three decades lived. The first spent with his mother. The second with friends. The third trying to build something. "接下去的十年，会是怎样呢？" — what will the next ten years be?

Business Maturity

Posts become more operational: inventory turnover cycles, pricing strategy (吊牌价 vs. 折扣), supply chain analysis. He visits competitors in Beijing, finds their operations much larger. His wife says his operation is "有些让人失望" (a bit disappointing). He buys his own trademark.

Personal Life Deepens

He gets married in 2011. He watches Good Will Hunting and calls it "free therapy." He watches Inception and decides the whole movie is a dream within a dream. His wife drags him to a modern dance performance (流浪者之歌); he goes reluctantly but is moved. The cat-loving, poetry-quoting young developer is becoming a businessman-husband.

Key posts

  • 对身边的人好一点 (Jan 2010) — Be nicer to the people around you. Half a life lived, not many more close relationships ahead.
  • 我们都寂寞 (Sep 2010) — A friend's status: "有人要进来，就必须要有一些人离开." Near middle age, a sudden pang of loneliness.

IV. The Searcher 2012–2013 · 57 posts

A philosophical resurgence. The business stabilizes enough that the mind starts wandering again — but this time, it wanders deeper.

Systems Thinking Emerges

建立生态比建立一个强劲的单体更重要 (Feb 2012) — Building an ecosystem is more important than building a strong single entity. He's describing Taobao's platform model, but the insight is universal. He revisits butterfly effects and starts questioning them. He re-opens Samuelson's economics and finds macroeconomics as counterintuitive as quantum physics.

Chinese Philosophy

He reads Feng Youlan's 中国哲学简史 (A Short History of Chinese Philosophy) in three installments, writing long reviews of each. He reads Qian Mu's 中国历代政治得失 (Political Gains and Losses Through Chinese Dynasties). He formulates his own philosophy: "置身事内，置心世外" — feet on the ground, mind above the world. A synthesis of Confucian engagement and Daoist detachment.

Fatherhood

On June 16, 2013, his son 子墨 (Zimo) is born. His first Father's Day. The naming process gets its own post — considering clan genealogy, the mother's surname, and phonetic beauty. A few weeks later: "终于，敢拍两张子墨的照片啦" — finally brave enough to photograph the baby.

A Letter to His Son

In "说说历史这学科" (Sep 2013), he writes a letter to infant Zimo about why history matters, how written records enable cross-temporal spiritual exchange, and why understanding human nature through history is as important as understanding science.

Key posts

  • 淘宝宅男日记《看清真实的市场》 (May 2012) — Using a paid analytics tool to discover that most successful Taobao shops aren't nearly as profitable as they claim. "马云是个大忽悠" — Jack Ma is a great bluffer.
  • 提倡『慢创业』 (Jun 2012) — Advocating "slow entrepreneurship." The government pushed fast startups; he argues for patience, learning, and organic growth.
  • 厉害了，哲学观 (Oct 2013) — "置身事内，置心世外" — his personal philosophical synthesis.

V. The Pre-Academic 2014–2016 · 23 posts

Writing becomes sparse but each post carries more weight. The intellectual transformation is unmistakable.

Science and Complexity

He reads 时间简史 (A Brief History of Time), 异类 (Outliers), 演化 (Evolution). Back in 2012, he'd already discovered that his 2008 intuition about "matter as vibration" has a name: string theory ("原来它叫做超弦理论"). Now the reading deepens. He writes about Buddhism as a philosophical system (not religion), and the failings of mechanical education models.

论 蝴蝶效应是个谬论 (Jul 2015) — He writes a careful argument against the butterfly effect, foreshadowing his later interest in complex systems and computational irreducibility. The same person who once studied Taobao ranking algorithms is now reasoning about nonlinear dynamics.

Reading Bertalanffy

The penultimate intellectual milestone: "颠覆三观，读《生命问题》冯·贝塔朗菲" (Feb 2016) — reading the founder of general systems theory. He'd previously concluded that "life is just a manifestation of system complexity." Now he finds the theoretical foundation for it. This is the bridge to his later academic path in complex systems.

美国观感

美国观感 (Aug 2016) — His second trip to America. Nine observations on urban planning, public infrastructure, traffic rules, race relations, food, housing, clothing, and weather. Written with the detailed eye of someone beginning to see America not just as a destination but as a possible home. The last Qzone post. The next chapter — graduate school, complex systems, a new country — is still a few years away, but the trajectory is set.

Key posts

  • 虚拟的将比现实的更现实 (Mar 2015) — The virtual will become more real than reality. Written about cloud servers, but prophetic for his later work on LLM internals.
  • 孩子总考100分，家长该怎么办？ (Apr 2016) — What should parents do when their kid always scores 100? An argument against mechanical education and for nurturing curiosity. Written as a father, read as a future academic.

The Arc

2007: A young builder — coding PHP, designing websites, reading economics, quoting Zhuangzi between beer sessions.

2008–09: Studio by day, Taobao by night — the diaries begin, the business takes shape, learning by doing.

2010–11: A mature operator — warehouse, marriage, brand-building, slowing down to think about what matters.

2012–13: A philosopher-father — Chinese philosophy, fatherhood, systems thinking, "slow entrepreneurship."

2014–16: A proto-scientist — physics, evolution, general systems theory, complexity. The last post is about his second trip to America, a few years before the move.

The throughline is unmistakable: a mind that was always asking "why does this system work this way?" — first about PHP templates, then about Taobao's ranking algorithm, then about Chinese governance, then about quantum physics, then about life itself. The interests changed; the question never did.
