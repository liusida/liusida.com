I. The Public Thinker 2014–2018 · science, systems, first principles

The man who arrives on WeChat Moments is no longer the hustling young builder of Early Years. He still carries the same instinct — to ask what lies beneath the surface of things — but the medium has changed his expression. The posts are shorter, sharper, and more portable. They read less like blog essays and more like intellectual field notes: quick attempts to grasp the hidden logic of medicine, genetics, AI, mathematics, power, enterprise, and education.

One of the first long posts, 《颠覆医疗》, treats medicine not as a sacred institution but as an imperfect system whose consumers often overestimate its reliability. Soon after comes 基因食物为什么不安全, where genetic engineering is reframed through complexity and unintended side effects. A few weeks later, in Watson 与奇点, he pushes back against overexcited timelines for artificial intelligence, concluding that the road from game-playing or quiz-winning machines to truly independent intelligence is still very long.

Again and again, the characteristic move is the same: take a fashionable topic, strip away its slogans, and rebuild it as a question of structure. In 引力波新闻, gravitational waves become not a headline but “a strange telescope” that sees mass rather than light. In 数学的用途之一, mathematics becomes a compression format for the wisdom of previous generations. In 毛估估的能力, even “拍脑袋” management is partially redeemed as a fast heuristic for navigating complexity. The old builder’s interest in systems has clearly survived the move from websites and commerce into science and thought.

Education also begins to emerge as a major concern. In 努力与天分, he pushes back against the fashionable habit of praising effort at all costs, noticing that “performative effort” can be misleading. In 如何防止小学生因为内容都会了而丧失兴趣, he borrows the logic of transfer learning to suggest that repetition need not be boring if one changes the angle. The machine learning mind is already being converted into a pedagogy.

Perhaps the clearest statement of temperament comes in 阿尔哈金与科学方法. The post is nominally historical, but its real purpose is self-definition. Suspicion, evidence, criticism of authority, and the duty to question even oneself — these are not just scientific virtues for him; they are a way of living.

数学，可以把前人的智慧用公式的形式保存起来供后人使用。
追寻真理的人……要把自己当作一个敌人。

Key posts

  • Watson 与奇点 — A sober correction to AI hype, already showing the long-run systems view.
  • 引力波新闻 — A public science event turned into a crisp conceptual model.
  • 如何防止小学生因为内容都会了而丧失兴趣 — One of the earliest posts where machine learning and pedagogy fuse.
  • 阿尔哈金与科学方法 — Less a historical note than a personal credo.

II. The Father as Learning Architect 2019–2021 · parenting, pedagogy, translation

The center of gravity shifts in these years. Fatherhood is no longer background context; it becomes method. The child is not only loved, but observed, challenged, protected, and carefully introduced to difficult things. The posts start to read like dispatches from a private school with one teacher, one student, and an unusually ambitious curriculum.

There is a special tenderness to the way he treats learning at home. In 文科生思维学数学, he tries to see whether his son can discover hidden rules through examples before formal explanation is given. In 八个 digit 排出几个 number, a painful little domestic moment becomes an educational revelation: after the child works for more than an hour to derive a combinatorial result, the father too casually types 8! into a calculator and accidentally makes the whole struggle feel trivial. The child bursts into tears. The father immediately understands the error. It is not about the factorial sign. It is about never allowing elegance to humiliate effort.

This kind of moment matters because it shows what distinguishes these posts from ordinary parenting reflections. He is not simply recording family life. He is building an ethics of teaching. A good teacher, in this world, is not someone who knows shortcuts, but someone who understands when not to reveal them too early.

Meanwhile, broader reflections on science, society, and language continue to deepen. In 为什么国内科学这事挺难搞, he argues that the deepest barrier to scientific participation is not national character or cultural inferiority but language: the shoulders of modern giants are written in English, and one cannot stand on them without first reading them. It is a clean, unsentimental, and persuasive observation — very much in his style.

By now, the themes of the archive are unmistakable. Learning matters more than performance. Structure matters more than slogan. Transmission matters more than display. And technology is always interesting, but only insofar as it changes what human beings can understand and hand to one another.

千万别让别人在特别有成就感的时候一盆冷水觉得白忙活了。
最关键的原因，其实是语言障碍。

Key posts

  • 文科生思维学数学 — A small domestic experiment that reveals a larger philosophy of teaching.
  • 为什么国内科学这事挺难搞 — A sharp argument about language and access to modern knowledge.
  • 八个 digit 排出几个 number — One of the most memorable posts in the archive, because it is both mathematical and moral.

III. The Math Household 2022–2023 · mathematics, cognition, learning science

If one subject dominates the middle of the WeChat archive, it is not AI but learning — especially mathematical learning. These may be the most original posts in the whole collection, not because they are formal scholarship, but because they show a serious mind constructing its own pedagogy in public.

The household becomes a miniature laboratory of mathematical culture. In 什么是数学, a child’s simple question opens onto a distinction between pure mathematics and science: pure mathematics explores an ideal, perfect world, while science explores the real one. In 建立数字大小的感觉, number sense is cultivated step by step, with careful control of difficulty. In X 像打猎, algebra becomes a game of hunting for an unknown animal. In 五年级小客人与证明, he notices how the mere presence of an “不一定” option can destabilize confidence, revealing the difference between intuition and proof.

These are not isolated anecdotes. They are parts of a coherent worldview. Math, in this archive, is not a school subject. It is a training ground for abstraction, proof, number sense, intellectual honesty, and delight. School often appears here as a narrow and lossy interface to learning: useful at times, but always much smaller than the thing it claims to represent.

That idea becomes explicit in some of the strongest posts of this period. In 学习和上课, he argues that attending class is only a tiny subset of learning. In 数学和数学课, he makes the parallel claim that math class is only a tiny subset of mathematics. In 为什么没人告诉学生学习是什么, he asks one of the central questions of the whole archive: why do students spend so much time “learning” without anyone first telling them what learning actually is?

The archive also widens beyond home experiments into reading and reflection. There are posts on Euclid, Bertrand Russell, Abraham Lincoln, Hardy, the beauty of mathematics, progressive education, working memory, semantic memory, and the psychology of practice. The tone is never that of a formal academic article, but the ambition is unmistakably larger than parental note-taking. These posts read like fragments of a future book.

纯数学是探索一个理想的完美世界，而纯科学是探索我们所属的现实世界。
学习和上课是不一样的，上课只是学习的很小的一个子集。
数学和数学课是不一样的，数学课只是数学的很小的一个子集。
为什么我们的学生们花了那么多的时间去“学习”，却没有人来告诉他们到底“学习”是什么？

Key posts

  • 什么是数学 — A child’s question answered with metaphysical seriousness and pedagogical charm.
  • 建立数字大小的感觉 — Teaching as fine craftsmanship: always placing the next step just within reach.
  • 学习和上课 — A succinct distinction with implications far beyond school.
  • 数学和数学课 — One of the clearest statements of the archive’s anti-reductionist educational philosophy.
  • 为什么没人告诉学生学习是什么 — The question at the heart of the whole pedagogy.

IV. The AI Naturalist 2023–2026 · LLMs, tools, society, autonomy

In the later posts, AI is no longer a distant subject of speculation. It enters ordinary life. ChatGPT helps explain electronics. Auto-GPT is followed like an unfolding drama. ComfyUI becomes a new way of seeing models. Tokenizers are examined like biological organs. GitHub turns into a kind of open-world game. What matters is not just the tools themselves, but the fact that they can now be brought into the household, into learning, into conversation, into the process of becoming a person.

He is especially good in these years at writing near-future social theory in miniature. In one post, he imagines a world where AIs communicate with each other on behalf of their owners. In another, he sketches a likely future in which every person is paired with a personal AI, and social capability becomes partly a function of that pairing and the compute behind it. The tone is not breathless. He is too experienced for that. It is observational, exploratory, and often a little amused.

Yet the deeper philosophical structure remains the same as before. He continues to return to complexity, embodiment, religion, science, verification, and the social effects of language and institutions. In 科学和玄学的区别, he insists that the line between science and mysticism is not imagination but verification: both may begin with speculation, but only one submits itself to careful testing. That sentence could stand as the intellectual motto of the whole WeChat archive.

The move to Singapore sharpens his observational writing again. Education, school culture, immigrant English, social trust, public order, multilingual life — the comparative eye that once wrote 美国观感 has returned, but now from inside a new society rather than outside it. At the same time, the father-son arc enters a new stage. By the time the boy is twelve, the posts increasingly acknowledge the need to step back. The father who once designed every learning condition now faces a harder task: respecting the child’s own will.

This becomes one of the quiet emotional resolutions of the archive. The earlier years were full of careful intervention; the later ones contain more release. Teaching, after all, cannot only be about shaping. It must also be about letting go.

基本每个人都搭配自己专属的AI。
科学和玄学的区别，并不在于谁在发挥想象……而是在于想象完了之后有没有小心求证。
如果有兴趣，跑几步又何妨。

Key posts

  • AI 代理之间的交流 — A compact vision of how everyday social life may be restructured by AI mediation.
  • 每个人的专属 AI — One of the clearest future-facing social sketches in the archive.
  • 科学和玄学的区别 — A concise statement of the archive’s epistemic ethics.
  • 如果有兴趣，跑几步又何妨 — The later voice at its best: encouraging, spacious, and less controlling than before.

The Arc

2014–2018: A public thinker — reading medicine, AI, mathematics, power, and education through the lens of systems and first principles.

2019–2021: A father as learning architect — turning parenting into curriculum design, and curriculum design into an ethics of care.

2022–2023: A math household — Euclid, proof, number sense, cognition, and the difference between learning and school.

2023–2026: An AI naturalist — domesticating new tools, imagining their social consequences, and slowly learning to let the next generation think for itself.

The throughline is unmistakable: a mind that keeps asking not merely what works, but why systems work the way they do — first in science and public ideas, then in teaching, then in mathematics, then in AI, and finally in the difficult art of raising a child without shrinking his freedom.

If Early Years was the story of a young man becoming capable in the world, Early-Year-2 is the story of a capable man trying to decide what capability is for. The answer he keeps circling is some combination of curiosity, transmission, rigor, and generosity: to understand the world more deeply, to hand better habits of thought to the next generation, and to make use of new tools without becoming spiritually smaller because of them.
